Information

The Basilica del Voto Nacional: A Neo-Gothic Gem in Quito The Basilica del Voto Nacional is one of the most impressive and iconic structures in Quito, Ecuador. This majestic Neo-Gothic religious building is not only a place of worship but also a tourist attraction, offering visitors a unique experience of architecture, history, and panoramic views. In this blog, we will explore the highlights of The Basilica del Voto Nacional.

ORIGINS AND CONSTRUCTION

The idea to build La Basilica del Voto Nacional arose as a monument commemorating the consecration of the Republic of Ecuador to the Sacred Heart of Jesus. The project was designed by French architect Emilio Tarlier, who was inspired by Notre Dame Cathedral in Paris. The plans were drawn up between 1890 and 1896, and construction began in 1883 and was completed in 1924.

INAUGURATION AND SIGNIFICANCE

The Basilica was officially inaugurated in 1924, although it is still considered "incomplete" according to Ecuadorian tradition, as it is said that if it is ever completely finished, the world will come to an end. This myth adds an aura of mystery and fascination to the monument.

AND DIMENSIONS

STYLE The Basilica del Voto Nacional is the largest Neo-Gothic temple in the Americas. With a length of 140 meters and its two main towers reaching a height of 115 meters, it is also the tallest Neo-Gothic church in South America. Its design and architectural details are a testament to the European influence on Ecuadorian religious architecture.

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENTS

One of the Basilica's most unique aspects is the inclusion of gargoyles and other decorative details depicting animals endemic to Ecuador, such as iguanas, turtles, and armadillos. This creates a unique connection between European Gothic architecture and the local biodiversity.

CHAPELS AND PANORAMIC VIEWS

THE CHAPELS The Basilica has 24 side chapels, each dedicated to one of Ecuador's provinces. These chapels not only serve as places of worship but also represent the country's unity and diversity.

TOWERS AND VIEWS

One of the biggest tourist attractions of the Basílica del Voto Nacional is the opportunity to climb its towers. From there, visitors can enjoy unparalleled panoramic views of Quito and its surroundings. The climb can be challenging, but the views are worth every effort. The towers can be accessed via a series of stairs and bridges, adding a touch of adventure to the visit.
